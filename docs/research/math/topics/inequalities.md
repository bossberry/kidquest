# Inequalities

## Purpose

Help children understand how quantities can be compared when values are not necessarily equal, building the foundation for algebra, graphing, optimization, and mathematical reasoning.

---

## Core Concepts

An inequality compares quantities that are:

- greater than
- less than
- greater than or equal to
- less than or equal to

Unlike equations, inequalities describe ranges of values rather than a single value.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Comparing Numbers

Examples:

7 > 4

2 < 9

Children understand relative size.

---

### Stage 2: Using Number Lines

Numbers farther right are greater.

Example:

-2 < 3

---

### Stage 3: Introducing Variables

Example:

x > 5

Any number larger than 5 satisfies the inequality.

---

### Stage 4: Multiple Solutions

Example:

x < 4

Solutions include:

3

0

-5

Unlike equations, inequalities often have many solutions.

---

### Stage 5: Graphing Inequalities

Example:

x ≥ 2

Closed circle at 2

Shade to the right.

---

### Stage 6: Solving Addition Inequalities

Example:

x + 3 > 8

Subtract 3:

x > 5

---

### Stage 7: Solving Multiplication Inequalities

Example:

2x < 10

Divide by 2:

x < 5

---

### Stage 8: Reversing the Symbol

Example:

-2x > 6

Divide by -2:

x < -3

Multiplying or dividing by a negative reverses the inequality.

---

## Solution Sets

Example:

x > 4

Possible solutions:

5

10

100

The solution set contains infinitely many values.

---

## Number Line Representation

Open circle:

Value not included.

Example:

x > 3

---

Closed circle:

Value included.

Example:

x ≥ 3

---

## Relationship with Equations

Equation:

x = 5

Single solution.

---

Inequality:

x > 5

Many solutions.

---

## Relationship with Graphing

Inequalities can be represented visually.

Number lines show all possible solutions.

Later, inequalities are graphed on coordinate planes.

## Common Misconceptions

### Inequalities Have One Solution

They usually describe many solutions.

---

### Open and Closed Circles Are the Same

Open circles exclude values.

Closed circles include values.

---

### Symbols Never Reverse

Multiplying or dividing by a negative reverses the symbol.

---

### Greater Than Means Bigger Digits

Negative numbers require careful comparison.

Example:

-2 > -5
