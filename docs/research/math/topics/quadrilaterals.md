# Quadrilaterals

## Purpose

Help children understand four-sided polygons and their properties, building the foundation for geometry, area, symmetry, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

A quadrilateral is a polygon with:

- 4 sides
- 4 vertices
- 4 angles

The sum of the interior angles of every quadrilateral is:

360°

Quadrilaterals can have different side lengths and angle measures.

## Types of Quadrilaterals

### Square

Properties:

- 4 equal sides
- 4 right angles

Lines of symmetry:

4

---

### Rectangle

Properties:

- opposite sides equal
- 4 right angles

Lines of symmetry:

2

---

### Rhombus

Properties:

- 4 equal sides

Angles may vary.

---

### Parallelogram

Properties:

- opposite sides parallel
- opposite sides equal

---

### Trapezoid

Properties:

- one pair of parallel sides

---

### Kite

Properties:

- two pairs of adjacent equal sides

---

## Interior Angle Sum

Every quadrilateral has interior angles that add to:

360°

Example:

90° + 90° + 90° + 90°

= 360°

---

## Relationship with Triangles

A quadrilateral can be divided into two triangles.

Since each triangle has:

180°

The quadrilateral has:

360°

---

## Relationship with Area

Examples:

Rectangle

Area = length × width

---

Square

Area = side × side

---

Parallelogram

Area = base × height

---

## Relationship with Symmetry

Square:

4 lines of symmetry

Rectangle:

2 lines of symmetry

Some quadrilaterals have no lines of symmetry.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Squares Are Not Rectangles

Mathematically:

Every square is a rectangle.

---

### Bigger Shapes Have Larger Angles

Size does not determine angle measures.

---

### Rotated Shapes Become Different Shapes

Orientation does not change classification.

---

### All Quadrilaterals Have Equal Sides

Only some quadrilaterals do.
