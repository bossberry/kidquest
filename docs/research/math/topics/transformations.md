# Transformations

## Purpose

Help children understand how shapes can move, flip, turn, and resize while preserving or changing certain properties, building the foundation for geometry, symmetry, coordinate geometry, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

Transformations change the position, orientation, or size of shapes.

Common transformations include:

- translation
- reflection
- rotation
- dilation

Transformations help describe movement and relationships between shapes.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Sliding Shapes

Children move shapes without changing size or orientation.

This movement is called a translation.

---

### Stage 2: Flipping Shapes

Shapes can be reflected across a line.

Reflections create mirror images.

---

### Stage 3: Turning Shapes

Shapes rotate around a point.

Examples:

- quarter turn (90°)
- half turn (180°)
- full turn (360°)

---

### Stage 4: Enlarging and Reducing

Shapes can change size.

This transformation is called dilation.

Examples:

- maps
- photographs

---

### Stage 5: Identifying Transformations

Children determine whether a shape has been:

- translated
- reflected
- rotated
- dilated

---

### Stage 6: Symmetry

Reflections create symmetrical figures.

Lines of symmetry are closely related to transformations.

---

### Stage 7: Coordinate Transformations

Transformations can be described using coordinates.

Example:

Point:

(2,3)

Translated right by 4 units:

(6,3)

---

## Translation

A translation slides a shape.

Properties preserved:

- side lengths
- angle measures
- orientation

Shapes remain congruent.

---

## Reflection

A reflection flips a shape across a line.

Properties preserved:

- lengths
- angles

Orientation changes.

---

## Rotation

A rotation turns a shape around a point.

Properties preserved:

- lengths
- angles

Examples:

90°

180°

270°

---

## Dilation

A dilation changes size.

Properties preserved:

- shape
- angle measures

Side lengths change according to the scale factor.

---

## Congruence and Similarity

### Congruent Shapes

Same size and shape.

Produced by:

- translations
- reflections
- rotations

---

### Similar Shapes

Same shape, different size.

Produced by:

- dilations

---

## Relationship with Symmetry

Reflections create mirror symmetry.

Rotations create rotational symmetry.

---

## Relationship with Coordinate Planes

Coordinates provide precise descriptions of transformations.

Example:

Translation:

(1,2) → (4,2)

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Rotation Changes Shape

Rotation changes orientation, not shape.

---

### Reflection Changes Size

Reflections preserve size.

---

### Translation Means Rotation

Sliding and turning are different transformations.

---

### Dilation Produces Congruent Shapes

Dilations create similar shapes, not congruent shapes.

- mirrors

"What changed? What stayed the same?"
