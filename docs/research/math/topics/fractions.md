# Fractions

## Purpose

Help children understand that a fraction represents equal parts of a whole.

---

## Core Concepts

### Whole

One complete object.

Examples:

- One pizza
- One cake
- One apple

---

### Equal Parts

Fractions only work when parts are equal.

Correct:

- Pizza cut into 4 equal slices

Incorrect:

- Unequal slices

---

### Numerator and Denominator

Example:

1/4

- Numerator (1): number of parts taken.
- Denominator (4): total equal parts.

---

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Half

Examples:

- Half an apple
- Half a sandwich

Vocabulary:

- half
- one-half
- equal parts

---

### Stage 2: Fourths

Examples:

- Quarter of a pizza
- Quarter of a chocolate bar

Vocabulary:

- quarter
- fourth
- one-fourth

---

### Stage 3: Thirds

Examples:

- Divide three cookies equally.
- Three children share one cake.

Vocabulary:

- third
- one-third

---

### Stage 4: Compare Fractions

Which is larger?

1/2 or 1/4

Children discover:

1/2 > 1/4

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Bigger denominator means bigger amount

Incorrect:

1/8 > 1/4

Correct:

1/8 is smaller.

---

### Unequal pieces still represent fractions

Fractions require equal parts.

---
