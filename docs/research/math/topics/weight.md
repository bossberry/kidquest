# Weight

## Purpose

Help children understand and measure how heavy objects are, developing the foundation for measurement, science, estimation, and everyday problem solving.

---

## Core Concepts

Weight describes how heavy something is.

Children learn to:

- compare objects
- estimate weight
- use measuring tools
- understand standard units

Examples:

- heavier
- lighter
- same weight

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Comparing Objects

Examples:

Which object is heavier?

Which object is lighter?

Children use their hands to compare.

---

### Stage 2: Nonstandard Units

Measure weight using:

- blocks
- marbles
- counters

Example:

The toy weighs as much as 8 cubes.

---

### Stage 3: Understanding Standard Units

Common units:

- gram (g)
- kilogram (kg)

---

### Stage 4: Using Scales

Children use:

- balance scales
- digital scales

They learn to read measurements accurately.

---

### Stage 5: Estimation

Examples:

An apple weighs about 200 grams.

A watermelon weighs several kilograms.

---

### Stage 6: Comparing Measurements

Examples:

500 g and 700 g

700 g is heavier.

---

### Stage 7: Unit Relationships

1,000 grams = 1 kilogram

Children understand the connection between units.

---

## Standard Units

### Gram (g)

Used for light objects.

Examples:

- paper clips
- cookies
- pencils

---

### Kilogram (kg)

Used for heavier objects.

Examples:

- backpacks
- pets
- people

---

## Measuring Tools

### Balance Scale

Compares two weights.

---

### Spring Scale

Measures force and weight.

---

### Digital Scale

Provides numerical measurements.

## Estimation Benchmarks

Examples:

Paper clip

≈ 1 gram

Apple

≈ 200 grams

Bag of rice

≈ 1 kilogram

Adult person

≈ 50–100 kilograms

Benchmarks help children develop measurement sense.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Bigger Objects Are Always Heavier

Example:

A large balloon may weigh less than a small rock.

---

### Weight and Size Mean the Same Thing

Large objects are not necessarily heavy.

---

### Confusing Grams and Kilograms

Example:

5 kg is heavier than 500 g.

---

### Ignoring Units

100 and 100 kg are very different quantities.

Units matter.
