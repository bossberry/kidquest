# Place Value

## Purpose

Help children understand that the value of a digit depends on its position within a number, forming the foundation for arithmetic, decimals, and algebra.

---

## Core Concepts

Place value means that the same digit can represent different amounts depending on where it appears.

Examples:

- 3 ones = 3
- 3 tens = 30
- 3 hundreds = 300

Position determines value.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Ones

Single objects represent one unit.

Examples:

7

Means:

7 ones

---

### Stage 2: Tens

Ten ones form one ten.

Example:

10

1 ten

0 ones

---

### Stage 3: Tens and Ones

Example:

24

2 tens

4 ones

20 + 4

---

### Stage 4: Hundreds

Ten tens form one hundred.

Example:

346

3 hundreds

4 tens

6 ones

300 + 40 + 6

---

### Stage 5: Thousands

Example:

2,583

2 thousands

5 hundreds

8 tens

3 ones

---

### Stage 6: Expanded Form

Example:

572

500 + 70 + 2

---

### Stage 7: Comparing Numbers

Example:

47 and 74

47

4 tens and 7 ones

74

7 tens and 4 ones

Therefore:

74 > 47

---

### Stage 8: Composing Numbers

Example:

3 hundreds

5 tens

2 ones

Number:

352

---

### Stage 9: Decomposing Numbers

Example:

684

600 + 80 + 4

## Base Ten System

Our number system uses groups of ten.

10 ones = 1 ten

10 tens = 1 hundred

10 hundreds = 1 thousand

This structure is called the base ten system.

## Common Misconceptions

### Reading Digits Separately

Incorrect:

52 means "5 and 2"

Correct:

52 means

5 tens and 2 ones

---

### Confusing Digit and Value

In 46:

Digit:

4

Value:

40

---

### Reversing Numbers

24 and 42 represent different quantities.

---

### Ignoring Zero

Example:

205

Means:

2 hundreds

0 tens

5 ones

Zero acts as a placeholder.

## Teacher Notes

Use concrete materials before introducing symbols.

Encourage children to explain:

"How many tens?"

"How many ones?"

Develop understanding rather than memorization.

## Parent Notes
