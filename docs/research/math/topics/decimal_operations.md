# Decimal Operations

## Purpose

Help children understand how to add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals, building the foundation for money, measurement, percentages, algebra, and scientific calculations.

---

## Core Concepts

Decimals represent parts of a whole using the base ten system.

Operations with decimals extend whole number operations and rely heavily on place value.

Examples:

- addition
- subtraction
- multiplication
- division

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Understanding Decimal Place Value

Examples:

0.1

0.25

3.75

Children understand:

- ones
- tenths
- hundredths

---

### Stage 2: Comparing Decimals

Examples:

0.8 > 0.5

1.25 > 1.2

Children use place value rather than digit count.

---

### Stage 3: Adding Decimals

Example:

2.3 + 1.4

Align decimal points.

Answer:

3.7

---

### Stage 4: Subtracting Decimals

Example:

5.8 − 2.3

Answer:

3.5

---

### Stage 5: Multiplying Decimals by Whole Numbers

Example:

2.5 × 4

Answer:

10

---

### Stage 6: Multiplying Decimals by Decimals

Example:

0.5 × 0.2

Answer:

0.1

Children understand multiplication as scaling.

---

### Stage 7: Dividing Decimals

Example:

2.4 ÷ 0.6

Answer:

4

Division determines how many groups fit.

---

### Stage 8: Estimation

Example:

3.98 + 5.12

Estimate:

4 + 5

≈ 9

Exact answer:

9.10

---

## Addition of Decimals

Align decimal points.

Example:

1.25

- 2.40

= 3.65

Place value determines alignment.

---

## Subtraction of Decimals

Example:

6.80

− 2.35

= 4.45

---

## Multiplication of Decimals

Example:

1.2 × 3

= 3.6

---

Example:

0.5 × 0.5

= 0.25

Multiplication can produce smaller values.

---

## Division of Decimals

Example:

4.5 ÷ 1.5

= 3

---

Example:

0.8 ÷ 0.2

= 4

---

## Relationship with Fractions

Examples:

0.5 = 1/2

0.25 = 1/4

0.75 = 3/4

Decimals and fractions represent the same quantities in different forms.

---

## Relationship with Percentages

Examples:

0.50 = 50%

0.25 = 25%

0.10 = 10%

---

## Relationship with Money

Examples:

$3.50

$1.25

Decimals are widely used in financial situations.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Ignoring Decimal Alignment

Incorrect:

2.5

- 0.75

= 0.100

Correct:

3.25

---

### More Digits Mean Larger Numbers

Incorrect:

0.15 > 0.8

Correct:

0.15 < 0.8

---

### Multiplication Always Makes Numbers Bigger

Example:

0.5 × 0.5

= 0.25

---

### Division Always Makes Numbers Smaller

Example:

0.8 ÷ 0.2

= 4
