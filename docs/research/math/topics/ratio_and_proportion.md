# Ratio and Proportion

## Purpose

Help children understand relationships between quantities and develop the foundation for fractions, percentages, scaling, algebra, and real-world problem solving.

---

## Core Concepts

A ratio compares two quantities.

Examples:

2 red marbles and 3 blue marbles

Ratio:

2 : 3

A proportion states that two ratios are equal.

Example:

1 : 2 = 2 : 4

Both represent the same relationship.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Comparing Quantities

Example:

3 apples and 2 oranges

Ratio:

3 : 2

Children describe relationships rather than totals.

---

### Stage 2: Part-to-Part Ratios

Example:

4 boys and 6 girls

Ratio:

4 : 6

---

### Stage 3: Part-to-Whole Ratios

Example:

4 red blocks and 6 blue blocks

Total:

10 blocks

Ratio of red blocks to total:

4 : 10

---

### Stage 4: Equivalent Ratios

Example:

1 : 2

2 : 4

3 : 6

These ratios describe the same relationship.

---

### Stage 5: Scaling

Example:

Recipe for 2 people:

2 cups of rice

For 4 people:

4 cups of rice

The ratio remains the same.

---

### Stage 6: Understanding Proportion

Example:

2 : 3 = 4 : 6

Children recognize equal relationships.

---

### Stage 7: Missing Values

Example:

1 : 5 = 2 : ?

Answer:

10

---

## Types of Ratios

### Part-to-Part

Example:

2 cats and 5 dogs

Ratio:

2 : 5

---

### Part-to-Whole

Example:

2 cats and 5 dogs

Total:

7 animals

Ratio:

2 : 7

---

### Unit Rate

Example:

60 kilometers in 2 hours

Rate:

30 kilometers per hour

---

## Relationship with Fractions

Ratio:

1 : 4

can be represented as

1/4

Ratios and fractions both describe relationships.

---

## Relationship with Percentages

Example:

25 out of 100

Ratio:

25 : 100

Fraction:

1/4

Percentage:

25%

---

## Relationship with Scaling

Example:

Map scale

1 cm represents 10 km

Scale models preserve proportions.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Ratios Describe Totals

Ratios compare quantities, not totals.

---

### Order Does Not Matter

2 : 3 is different from 3 : 2.

Order is important.

---

### Equivalent Ratios Must Use the Same Numbers

1 : 2 and 2 : 4 represent the same relationship.

---

### Ratios and Fractions Are Identical

They are closely related but used differently.
