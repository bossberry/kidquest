# Neural Networks

## Purpose

Help children understand how interconnected mathematical structures can learn patterns from data, building the foundation for deep learning, computer vision, natural language processing, and modern artificial intelligence.

---

## Core Concepts

Neural networks are computational models inspired by the interconnected structure of the brain.

They learn patterns by adjusting internal connections.

Neural networks are composed of layers of simple units working together.

Modern AI systems are largely based on neural networks.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Inputs and Outputs

Example:

Picture

↓

Neural network

↓

Prediction

Inputs are transformed into outputs.

---

### Stage 2: Artificial Neurons

Artificial neurons receive information and produce outputs.

Many neurons working together create complex behavior.

---

### Stage 3: Layers

Neural networks contain:

- input layer
- hidden layers
- output layer

Information flows through these layers.

---

### Stage 4: Weights

Connections between neurons have weights.

Learning adjusts these weights.

---

### Stage 5: Training

Networks improve by learning from examples.

Errors help guide adjustments.

---

### Stage 6: Hidden Layers

Hidden layers discover useful patterns automatically.

Complex networks contain many hidden layers.

---

### Stage 7: Deep Learning

Networks with many layers are called deep neural networks.

Deep learning enables advanced AI systems.

---

### Stage 8: Modern AI

Examples:

- image recognition
- translation
- speech recognition
- large language models
