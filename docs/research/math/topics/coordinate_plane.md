# Coordinate Plane

## Purpose

Help children understand location, position, and graphing, building the foundation for geometry, algebra, functions, and data representation.

---

## Core Concepts

A coordinate plane is a system used to locate points using pairs of numbers.

Coordinates are written as:

(x, y)

Example:

(3, 2)

means:

- move 3 units horizontally
- move 2 units vertically

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Position and Location

Children describe positions.

Examples:

- above
- below
- left
- right

---

### Stage 2: Grids

Use rows and columns.

Examples:

Treasure maps

Board games

Chess boards

---

### Stage 3: Understanding Axes

Horizontal axis:

x-axis

Vertical axis:

y-axis

---

### Stage 4: The Origin

The point where the axes meet.

Coordinate:

(0, 0)

Called the origin.

---

### Stage 5: Plotting Points

Example:

(2, 4)

Move:

2 units right

4 units up

Plot the point.

---

### Stage 6: Reading Coordinates

Given a point on a graph, determine its coordinates.

---

### Stage 7: Multiple Points

Plot several locations.

Example:

A(1,2)

B(4,3)

C(2,5)

---

### Stage 8: Patterns on the Plane

Observe relationships between points.

Example:

(1,1)

(2,2)

(3,3)

These points form a straight line.

---

### Stage 9: Introduction to Quadrants

Coordinate planes have four quadrants.

Young learners often begin with only the first quadrant.

## Ordered Pairs

Order matters.

Example:

(2,5)

is different from

(5,2)

The first number represents x.

The second number represents y.

## Common Misconceptions

### Reversing Coordinates

Incorrect:

(3,5) and (5,3) are the same.

Correct:

They represent different points.

---

### Confusing x-axis and y-axis

Remember:

x comes first.

---

### Ignoring the Origin

All measurements begin from the origin.

---

### Believing Coordinates Are Random Numbers

Coordinates describe locations.
