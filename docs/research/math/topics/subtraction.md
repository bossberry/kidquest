# Subtraction

## Purpose

Help children understand that subtraction means taking away, finding the difference, or determining what remains.

---

## Core Concepts

Subtraction means:

- taking away
- removing objects
- comparing quantities
- finding missing parts

Examples:

5 − 2 = 3

8 − 3 = 5

---

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Taking Away Objects

Children physically remove objects.

Example:

🍎🍎🍎🍎🍎

Take away 🍎🍎

Remaining:

🍎🍎🍎

Answer:

3

---

### Stage 2: Count Back Strategy

Example:

7 − 2

Start at 7.

Count backward:

6, 5

Answer:

5

---

### Stage 3: Using Number Lines

Example:

8 − 3

Start at 8.

Move backward three spaces.

Answer:

5

---

### Stage 4: Finding Missing Parts

Example:

5 + ? = 9

Answer:

4

Therefore:

9 − 5 = 4

---

### Stage 5: Counting Up Strategy

Example:

9 − 7

Count upward:

7 → 8 → 9

Two jumps

Answer:

2

---

### Stage 6: Part-Part-Whole

Whole:

10

Parts:

6 and 4

Therefore:

10 − 6 = 4

10 − 4 = 6

---

### Stage 7: Subtracting Across Ten

Example:

13 − 5

Break apart:

13 − 3 = 10

10 − 2 = 8

Answer:

8

---

## Relationship with Addition

Addition and subtraction are inverse operations.

Example:

6 + 4 = 10

10 − 6 = 4

10 − 4 = 6

Fact families help children see these connections.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Bigger number must always come first

Children may think:

3 − 8 = 5

Correct:

Three is smaller than eight.

---

### Confusing subtraction with addition

Example:

7 − 2 = 9

Correct answer:

5

---

### Counting mistakes

Children may skip numbers while counting backward.

---

### Believing subtraction always means "taking away"

Subtraction can also mean:

- comparing amounts
- finding missing numbers

---

## Hands-on Activities

### Toy Removal

Start with several toys.

Remove some.

Count what remains.

---

### Finger Counting

Fold fingers down.

Count remaining fingers.

---

### Number Line Game

Move backward on a floor number line.

---

### Dice Difference

Roll two dice.

Find the difference.

---

### Building Blocks

Build towers of different heights.

Find how many blocks taller one tower is.

---

## Teacher Notes

Begin with concrete objects.

Encourage multiple strategies:

- taking away
- counting back
- counting up
- part-part-whole

Build understanding before memorization.

---

## Parent Notes

Use daily situations:

- snacks
- toys
- coins
- books

Ask:

"How many are left?"

"How many more do we need?"
