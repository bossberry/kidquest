# Symmetry

## Purpose

Help children understand balance and mirror images, developing spatial reasoning and laying the foundation for geometry, art, and coordinate systems.

---

## Core Concepts

Symmetry means that two parts of an object or figure match each other.

A line of symmetry divides a shape into two matching halves.

If one half is folded over the line of symmetry, it fits exactly on the other half.

## Common Misconceptions

### Same Size Means Symmetry

Two equal parts must also be mirror images.

---

### Rotation Changes Symmetry

Turning a shape does not change whether it is symmetrical.

---

### Every Shape Has Symmetry

Some shapes have no lines of symmetry.

---

### Unequal Halves Are Symmetrical

Symmetrical halves must match exactly.

## Teacher Notes

Use concrete experiences and mirrors.

Encourage children to explain:

"Why do these halves match?"

Avoid relying only on memorization.
