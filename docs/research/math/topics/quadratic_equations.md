# Quadratic Equations

## Purpose

Help children understand equations involving squared variables, building the foundation for graphing, functions, algebra, physics, and calculus.

---

## Core Concepts

A quadratic equation is an equation whose highest exponent is 2.

General form:

ax² + bx + c = 0

where:

- a ≠ 0
- a, b, and c are constants

Quadratic equations often have two solutions.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Recognizing Quadratics

Examples:

x² = 9

x² + 3x + 2 = 0

2x² − x + 5 = 0

Highest exponent is 2.

---

### Stage 2: Solving Simple Quadratics

Example:

x² = 16

Solutions:

x = 4

x = -4

---

### Stage 3: Factoring

Example:

x² + 5x + 6 = 0

Factored form:

(x + 2)(x + 3) = 0

Solutions:

x = -2

x = -3

---

### Stage 4: Graphing Quadratics

Example:

y = x²

Produces a U-shaped curve called a parabola.

---

### Stage 5: Vertex

Example:

y = (x − 2)² + 1

Vertex:

(2,1)

The vertex is the turning point.

---

### Stage 6: Axis of Symmetry

Example:

y = x²

Axis of symmetry:

x = 0

---

### Stage 7: Quadratic Formula

General equation:

ax² + bx + c = 0

Solutions can always be found using the quadratic formula.

---

### Stage 8: Discriminant

The discriminant determines the number of solutions.

Possible cases:

- two solutions
- one solution
- no real solutions

---

## Parabolas

Quadratic functions produce parabolas.

Examples:

y = x²

y = x² + 3

y = -x²

Parabolas may open upward or downward.

---

## Roots

Roots are x-values where:

y = 0

Example:

x² − 9 = 0

Roots:

x = 3

x = -3

---

## Relationship with Factoring

Example:

x² + 7x + 12

Factored form:

(x + 3)(x + 4)

Roots:

x = -3

x = -4

---

## Relationship with Graphing

Quadratic equations correspond to parabolas.

Roots correspond to x-intercepts.

---

## Relationship with Physics

Examples:

- projectile motion
- falling objects
- ball trajectories

Quadratic relationships occur naturally.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Quadratics Always Have Two Different Solutions

Some have one solution.

Some have no real solutions.

---

### Negative Numbers Cannot Be Solutions

Negative values may satisfy quadratic equations.

---

### All Parabolas Open Upward

Some open downward.

---

### Roots and Vertices Are the Same

Roots are x-intercepts.

Vertices are turning points.
