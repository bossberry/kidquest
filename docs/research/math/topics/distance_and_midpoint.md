# Distance and Midpoint

## Purpose

Help children understand how to measure distances and locate halfway points on coordinate planes, building the foundation for geometry, graphing, vectors, and analytic geometry.

---

## Core Concepts

Distance measures how far apart two points are.

A midpoint is the point exactly halfway between two points.

Distance and midpoint concepts connect geometry and algebra.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Understanding Near and Far

Children compare:

- near
- far
- closer
- farther

Distance describes separation.

---

### Stage 2: Measuring Line Segments

Examples:

- ruler measurements
- string measurements

Children understand distance as length.

---

### Stage 3: Points on a Coordinate Plane

Example:

A(2,3)

B(6,3)

Distance can be measured between points.

---

### Stage 4: Horizontal Distance

Example:

A(2,4)

B(7,4)

Distance:

7 − 2 = 5 units

---

### Stage 5: Vertical Distance

Example:

A(3,2)

B(3,8)

Distance:

8 − 2 = 6 units

---

### Stage 6: Midpoints on Number Lines

Example:

Endpoints:

2 and 8

Midpoint:

5

The midpoint lies halfway.

---

### Stage 7: Midpoints on Coordinate Planes

Example:

A(2,4)

B(8,4)

Midpoint:

(5,4)

Coordinates are averaged.

---

### Stage 8: General Midpoint Formula

For points:

(x₁, y₁)

(x₂, y₂)

The midpoint is obtained by averaging the coordinates.

## Distance

Distance measures how far apart two points are.

Examples:

- road lengths
- map distances
- running tracks

Distance is always nonnegative.

---

## Midpoint

The midpoint divides a segment into two equal parts.

Examples:

- center of a bridge
- halfway point on a trip
- middle of a ruler

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Midpoint Means Half the Distance from Zero

The midpoint lies halfway between two endpoints, not halfway from the origin.

---

### Distance Can Be Negative

Distance is always positive or zero.

---

### Coordinates Are Added Instead of Averaged

Midpoints require averaging coordinates.

---

### Horizontal and Vertical Distances Use Different Ideas

Both are measurements of separation.
