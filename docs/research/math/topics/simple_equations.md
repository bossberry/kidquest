# Simple Equations

## Purpose

Help children understand variables and equations as balanced relationships, building the foundation for algebra, functions, graphing, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

An equation is a mathematical statement showing that two quantities are equal.

Examples:

3 + 4 = 7

5 = 2 + 3

x + 2 = 5

Equations represent relationships, not simply instructions to calculate.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Understanding Equality

Examples:

4 + 1 = 5

5 = 3 + 2

Both sides represent the same amount.

---

### Stage 2: Missing Numbers

Example:

3 + ? = 7

Answer:

4

Children solve for unknown quantities.

---

### Stage 3: Variables

Variables represent unknown numbers.

Examples:

x

n

y

---

### Stage 4: One-Step Addition Equations

Example:

x + 4 = 9

Answer:

x = 5

---

### Stage 5: One-Step Subtraction Equations

Example:

x − 3 = 7

Answer:

x = 10

---

### Stage 6: One-Step Multiplication Equations

Example:

3x = 12

Answer:

x = 4

---

### Stage 7: One-Step Division Equations

Example:

x ÷ 5 = 4

Answer:

x = 20

---

### Stage 8: Multi-Step Equations

Example:

x + 3 = 10

Subtract 3 from both sides.

Answer:

x = 7

Children begin understanding inverse operations.

---

## Balance Concept

An equation behaves like a balance scale.

Example:

x + 2 = 7

Whatever is done to one side must also be done to the other side.

Balance must be preserved.

---

## Expressions vs Equations

### Expression

Example:

3x + 2

No equal sign.

---

### Equation

Example:

3x + 2 = 11

Contains an equal sign.

---

## Inverse Operations

Addition and subtraction are opposites.

Multiplication and division are opposites.

Examples:

x + 5 = 9

Subtract 5.

x = 4

---

3x = 15

Divide by 3.

x = 5

---

## Relationship with Number Bonds

Example:

8

Parts:

5 and 3

Therefore:

5 + 3 = 8

8 − 5 = 3

Equation solving builds on part-whole relationships.

---

## Relationship with Patterns

Example:

2, 4, 6, 8

Rule:

2 × n

Variables describe patterns.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Equal Sign Means "Write the Answer"

Incorrect:

3 + 2 = 5 + 1

Children may interpret the equal sign incorrectly.

The equal sign means both sides have the same value.

---

### Variables Are Objects

Variables represent numbers.

---

### Only One Side Changes

Operations must preserve balance.

---

### Guessing Without Reasoning

Children should use inverse operations and logical thinking.
