# Mental Math

## Purpose

Develop number sense and flexible thinking so children can solve problems efficiently without relying entirely on fingers, calculators, or written methods.

---

## Core Concepts

Mental math means solving problems in your head using patterns, number relationships, and efficient strategies.

Mental math is about understanding numbers, not memorizing procedures.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Subitizing

Instantly recognizing small quantities without counting.

Examples:

⚫⚫⚫

Children recognize "3" immediately.

---

### Stage 2: Counting On

Example:

5 + 3

Start at 5:

6, 7, 8

Answer:

8

---

### Stage 3: Counting Back

Example:

9 − 2

Count backward:

8, 7

Answer:

7

---

### Stage 4: Doubles

Examples:

1 + 1 = 2

4 + 4 = 8

7 + 7 = 14

10 + 10 = 20

---

### Stage 5: Near Doubles

Example:

6 + 7

Think:

6 + 6 + 1

Answer:

13

---

### Stage 6: Make Ten

Example:

8 + 5

Break 5 into 2 and 3.

8 + 2 = 10

10 + 3 = 13

---

### Stage 7: Compensation

Example:

19 + 6

Think:

20 + 6 = 26

26 − 1 = 25

Answer:

25

---

### Stage 8: Decomposition

Break numbers apart.

Example:

34 + 25

30 + 20 = 50

4 + 5 = 9

50 + 9 = 59

---

### Stage 9: Estimation

Example:

48 + 31

Round:

50 + 30

Estimate:

80

Exact answer:

79

---

## Number Sense Development

Children learn:

- numbers can be broken apart
- numbers can be rearranged
- multiple strategies can solve the same problem
- efficient methods save time

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Mental math means speed

Incorrect.

Mental math focuses on understanding, not racing.

---

### Only one strategy is correct

Different children may use different approaches.

---

### Fingers are bad

Finger counting is a normal developmental stage.

Children gradually move toward more efficient methods.

---

## Hands-on Activities

### Dot Cards

Flash dot patterns.

Children say the quantity instantly.

---

### Dice Games

Add numbers mentally.

---

### Number Talks

Ask:

"How did you get the answer?"

Encourage multiple solutions.

---

### Ten Frames

Visualize numbers and make ten.

---

### Card Games

Draw two cards and mentally add or subtract.

---

### Shopping Games

Estimate total prices.

## Difficulty Levels

Examples:

3 + 2

8 − 1

---

### Intermediate

Within 20

Examples:

9 + 8

---

### Advanced

Two-digit numbers

Examples:

27 + 16

43 − 18

---

## Teacher Notes

Encourage strategy sharing.

Avoid emphasizing speed competitions.

Focus on flexibility and understanding.

Accept multiple methods.

---

## Parent Notes

Practice naturally during daily life.

Examples:

- counting stairs
- adding toys
- comparing prices
- estimating waiting times

Keep activities playful rather than timed.
