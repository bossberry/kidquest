# Circles

## Purpose

Help children understand the properties and measurements of circles, building the foundation for geometry, coordinate systems, trigonometry, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

A circle is a set of points that are all the same distance from a center point.

Unlike polygons, circles have no sides and no vertices.

Examples:

- clocks
- wheels
- coins
- plates

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Recognizing Circles

Children identify circles in everyday life.

Examples:

- coins
- wheels
- clocks

---

### Stage 2: Understanding the Center

Every circle has a center point.

All points on the circle are equally distant from the center.

---

### Stage 3: Radius

The radius is the distance from the center to the circle.

Example:

Radius = 5 cm

---

### Stage 4: Diameter

The diameter passes through the center.

Relationship:

Diameter = 2 × radius

Example:

Radius = 4 cm

Diameter = 8 cm

---

### Stage 5: Circumference

Circumference measures the distance around the circle.

Examples:

- bicycle wheels
- racetracks

---

### Stage 6: Semicircles

A semicircle represents half of a circle.

Angle:

180°

---

### Stage 7: Quadrants

A circle can be divided into four equal parts.

Each part measures:

90°

---

### Stage 8: Area of Circles

Area measures the surface enclosed by the circle.

Area depends on the radius.

---

## Parts of a Circle

### Center

Middle point.

---

### Radius

Center to edge.

---

### Diameter

Edge to edge through the center.

Diameter = 2 × radius

---

### Circumference

Distance around the circle.

---

### Arc

Part of the circumference.

---

### Chord

Line segment connecting two points on the circle.

---

### Tangent

Line touching the circle at one point.

---

## Relationship Between Radius and Diameter

## Relationship with Fractions

Circle models help children understand:

- halves

Fractions are often visualized using circles.

---

## Relationship with Coordinate Geometry

Circles can be graphed using coordinate systems.

This leads to analytic geometry.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Larger Radius Means Larger Circumference Only Slightly

Circumference changes proportionally with radius.

---

### Diameter and Radius Are the Same

Diameter is twice the radius.

---

### Circles Have Sides

Circles have no straight sides.

---

### Orientation Changes a Circle

Circles look the same in every orientation.
