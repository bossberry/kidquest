# Continuity

## Purpose

Help children understand when functions behave smoothly without breaks, building the foundation for derivatives, integrals, and calculus.

---

## Core Concepts

A function is continuous if it can be drawn without lifting the pencil from the paper.

Continuous functions have no:

- holes
- jumps
- breaks

Continuity describes smooth behavior.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Continuous Paths

Examples:

- roads
- rivers
- strings

Children understand uninterrupted movement.

---

### Stage 2: Drawing Without Lifting

Some graphs can be drawn in one motion.

These graphs are continuous.

---

### Stage 3: Recognizing Breaks

Examples:

- missing points
- jumps
- gaps

These create discontinuities.

---

### Stage 4: Relationship with Limits

Continuity depends on limits.

A function is continuous when:

- the limit exists
- the function value exists
- they are equal

---

### Stage 5: Holes

Some functions have missing points.

The graph may approach the same value from both sides.

---

### Stage 6: Jump Discontinuities

Left and right sides approach different values.

A jump occurs.

---

### Stage 7: Infinite Discontinuities

Some functions grow without bound near certain points.

---

### Stage 8: Smooth Functions

Many functions used in calculus are continuous and smooth.

---

## Continuous Functions

Examples:

y = x

y = x²

y = sin(x)

These graphs have no interruptions.

---

## Types of Discontinuities

### Hole

A point is missing.

---

### Jump

The graph suddenly changes.

---

### Infinite Discontinuity

Values become extremely large.

---

## Relationship with Limits

Continuity relies on limits.

Without limits, continuity cannot be defined.

---

## Relationship with Graphs

Graphs help visualize:

- smoothness
- breaks
- holes

---

## Relationship with Derivatives

Differentiable functions are usually continuous.

Continuity comes before differentiation.

---

## Relationship with Integrals

Continuous functions are easier to integrate.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Continuous Means Straight

Curved graphs can also be continuous.

---

### Any Missing Point Destroys Nearby Behavior

Nearby values may still behave smoothly.

---

### Continuous Functions Cannot Cross Axes

Continuous functions may cross axes many times.

---

### Smooth Means Horizontal

Smoothness refers to absence of breaks.
