# Capacity

## Purpose

Help children understand how much a container can hold, developing the foundation for volume, measurement, science, and everyday problem solving.

---

## Core Concepts

Capacity describes how much liquid or material a container can hold.

Children learn to:

- compare containers
- estimate amounts
- use standard units
- measure accurately

Examples:

- more
- less
- full
- empty
- half-full

## Common Misconceptions

### Bigger Containers Always Hold More

Tall containers do not always have larger capacity.

Shape can be misleading.

---

### Volume and Capacity Are Identical

Capacity focuses on what a container can hold.

Volume describes the space occupied by an object.

---

### Ignoring Units

500 and 500 mL are different from 500 L.

Units matter.

---

### Confusing Weight and Capacity

A larger amount of liquid does not necessarily mean heavier objects in every context.
