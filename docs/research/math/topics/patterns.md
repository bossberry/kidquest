# Patterns

## Purpose

Help children recognize, describe, extend, and create patterns, forming the foundation for logical reasoning, algebra, and mathematical relationships.

---

## Core Concepts

A pattern is something that repeats or changes according to a rule.

Patterns help children:

- predict what comes next
- recognize relationships
- organize information
- develop algebraic thinking

## Common Misconceptions

### Looking Only at Objects

Children may focus on colors or shapes without recognizing the rule.

---

### Predicting Randomly

Children should explain why an answer follows the pattern.

---

### Assuming All Patterns Repeat

Some patterns grow instead of repeating.

Example:

1, 2, 3, 4

This is not a repeating pattern.

---

### Missing Multiple-Step Rules

Example:

2, 4, 8, 16

Rule:

Multiply by 2

Not:

Add 2

## Teacher Notes

Ask:

"What is the rule?"

"How do you know?"

Encourage explanation rather than guessing.

Use concrete materials before symbolic patterns.

---

## Parent Notes

Look for patterns during daily life.

Examples:

- floor tiles
- clothing
- songs
- routines

Encourage children to create their own patterns.
