# Computer Vision

## Purpose

Help children understand how computers can interpret and analyze images and videos, building the foundation for robotics, autonomous systems, medical imaging, and artificial intelligence.

---

## Core Concepts

Computer vision is the field of artificial intelligence that enables computers to understand visual information.

Computer vision helps machines:

- recognize objects
- detect faces
- interpret scenes
- analyze videos

Computer vision combines ideas from:

- mathematics
- geometry
- statistics
- machine learning
- neural networks

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Images as Information

Pictures contain information.

Computers represent images using numbers.

---

### Stage 2: Pixels

Images are made of tiny squares called pixels.

Each pixel contains color information.

---

### Stage 3: Patterns

Computers learn patterns inside images.

Examples:

- circles
- edges
- colors
- textures

---

### Stage 4: Classification

Example:

Image

↓

Cat or Dog

The computer predicts a category.

---

### Stage 5: Object Detection

Computers identify:

- what objects exist
- where they are located

---

### Stage 6: Segmentation

Images may be divided into meaningful regions.

Different objects are separated.

---

### Stage 7: Deep Learning

Neural networks improve image understanding.

Modern computer vision relies heavily on deep learning.

---

### Stage 8: Real-Time Vision

Computer vision allows machines to react to their environments.

---

## Pixels

Millions of pixels create detailed images.

## Common Misconceptions

### Computers See Like Humans

Computers process numbers rather than human experiences.

---

### Images Are Continuous

Images consist of discrete pixels.

---

### Recognition Is Perfect

Computer vision systems may make mistakes.

---

### Bigger Images Guarantee Better Recognition

Quality and training data also matter.
