# Coordinate Transformations

## Purpose

Help children understand how transformations can be represented on coordinate planes, building the foundation for geometry, algebra, graphing, and computer graphics.

---

## Core Concepts

Coordinate transformations describe how points move on a coordinate plane.

Common transformations include:

- translations
- reflections
- rotations
- dilations

Coordinates allow transformations to be described precisely.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Plotting Points

Examples:

(2,3)

(4,1)

Children understand coordinates as locations.

---

### Stage 2: Translations

Move points horizontally or vertically.

Example:

Point:

(3,2)

Move right 4 units.

New point:

(7,2)

---

Move up 3 units.

New point:

(3,5)

---

### Stage 3: Translation Rules

Horizontal movement:

(x + a, y)

Vertical movement:

(x, y + b)

---

Example:

(2,5)

Translate left 3 units.

(-1,5)

---

### Stage 4: Reflections

Reflect across the x-axis.

Example:

(4,3)

→

(4,-3)

---

Reflect across the y-axis.

Example:

(4,3)

→

(-4,3)

---

### Stage 5: Reflection Rules

Across x-axis:

(x, y)

→

(x, -y)

---

Across y-axis:

(x, y)

→

(-x, y)

---

### Stage 6: Rotations

Rotate about the origin.

Quarter turn:

90°

Half turn:

180°

Three-quarter turn:

270°

---

### Stage 7: Rotation Rules

90° counterclockwise:

(x, y)

→

(-y, x)

---

180°:

(x, y)

→

(-x, -y)

---

270° counterclockwise:

(x, y)

→

(y, -x)

---

### Stage 8: Dilations

Scale factor enlarges or reduces figures.

Example:

Scale factor = 2

(2,3)

→

(4,6)

---

Scale factor = ½

(4,6)

→

(2,3)

---

## Translation

Translations preserve:

- lengths
- angles
- orientation

Figures remain congruent.

---

## Reflection

Reflections preserve:

- lengths
- angles

Orientation changes.

---

## Rotation

Rotations preserve:

- lengths
- angles

Orientation changes.

---

## Dilation

Dilations preserve:

- shape
- angle measures

Size changes.

Figures become similar.

---

## Relationship with Symmetry

Reflections produce mirror symmetry.

Rotations produce rotational symmetry.

---

## Relationship with Algebra

Coordinate rules are algebraic expressions.

Example:

(x,y)

Translate right 5.

(x+5,y)

---

## Relationship with Graphing

Transformations move entire graphs.

Example:

y = x

Shift upward 2 units.

y = x + 2

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Translation Changes Shape

Only position changes.

---

### Reflection Changes Size

Reflections preserve size.

---

### Rotation Changes Lengths

Rotations preserve lengths.

---

### Dilations Produce Congruent Figures

Dilations produce similar figures.

---

### Rotation Direction Does Not Matter

Clockwise and counterclockwise rotations are different.
