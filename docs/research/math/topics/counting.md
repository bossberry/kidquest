# Counting

## Purpose

Help children develop number sense by learning to count objects, understand quantity, and recognize that numbers represent amounts.

---

## Core Concepts

Counting is the process of assigning number words to quantities.

Example:

🍎 🍎 🍎

Count:

1, 2, 3

There are 3 apples.

Counting is more than reciting numbers. Children must understand that the last number counted represents the total quantity.

## Principles of Counting

### One-to-One Correspondence

Each object receives one number word.

Correct:

🍎 → 1

🍎 → 2

🍎 → 3

---

### Stable Order

Numbers always appear in the same sequence.

1, 2, 3, 4, 5...

---

### Cardinality

The last number counted tells how many objects there are.

Example:

1, 2, 3, 4

Answer:

There are 4 objects.

---

### Abstraction

Anything can be counted:

- toys
- sounds
- jumps
- people

---

### Order Irrelevance

Objects can be counted in any order.

The total remains the same.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Rote Counting

Memorize number sequences.

Examples:

1–5

1–10

1–20

---

### Stage 2: Counting Objects

Match number words to objects.

Example:

🐻🐻🐻🐻

Answer:

4 bears

---

### Stage 3: Counting Forward

Examples:

6, 7, 8, 9, 10

---

### Stage 4: Counting Backward

Examples:

10, 9, 8, 7, 6

Backward counting prepares children for subtraction.

---

### Stage 5: Counting from Any Number

Start at:

7

Continue:

8, 9, 10

Children no longer need to begin at 1.

---

### Stage 6: Skip Counting

Count by:

2s

5s

10s

Examples:

2, 4, 6, 8

5, 10, 15, 20

Skip counting prepares children for multiplication.

---

### Stage 7: Counting Large Numbers

Examples:

25

50

100

1,000

---

## Comparing Quantities

Children learn:

- more
- less
- equal

Example:

4 apples and 6 oranges

6 is greater than 4.

---

## Subitizing

Instant recognition of small quantities without counting.

Examples:

⚫⚫⚫

Children immediately recognize 3.

Subitizing develops number sense.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Double Counting

Children count the same object twice.

---

### Skipping Objects

Children miss some objects.

---

### Forgetting Stable Order

Example:

1, 2, 4, 5

Missing:

3

---

### Believing Bigger Objects Mean Bigger Numbers

Five small objects are still more than three large objects.

---

### Recounting from One Every Time

Children gradually learn to count on from larger numbers.

## Teacher Notes

Counting should involve real objects.

Focus on understanding quantity, not memorization alone.

Develop cardinality and one-to-one correspondence early.

---

## Parent Notes

Use daily routines:

- climbing stairs
- setting the table
- putting away toys
- grocery shopping

Keep counting enjoyable and meaningful.
