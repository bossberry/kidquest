# Addition

## Purpose

Help children understand that addition means combining quantities to make a larger amount.

---

## Core Concepts

Addition means:

- putting together
- combining groups
- increasing amounts

Examples:

2 + 3 = 5

4 + 1 = 5

---

## Vocabulary

- add
- plus
- total
- altogether
- sum
- more

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Counting Objects

Children count physical objects.

Examples:

🍎🍎 + 🍎 = 🍎🍎🍎

---

### Stage 2: Count All Strategy

Example:

2 + 3

Count all:

1, 2, 3, 4, 5

Answer:

5

---

### Stage 3: Count On Strategy

Example:

3 + 2

Start at 3:

4, 5

Answer:

5

---

### Stage 4: Number Bonds

Examples:

5

- 2 + 3
- 1 + 4

10

- 7 + 3
- 6 + 4
- 5 + 5

---

### Stage 5: Doubles

Examples:

1 + 1 = 2

2 + 2 = 4

5 + 5 = 10

8 + 8 = 16

---

### Stage 6: Near Doubles

Examples:

5 + 6

Think:

5 + 5 + 1

Answer:

11

---

### Stage 7: Make Ten Strategy

Example:

8 + 5

Break apart 5:

8 + 2 + 3

10 + 3

Answer:

13

---

### Stage 8: Missing Addends

Example:

3 + ? = 8

Answer:

5

---

## Properties of Addition

### Commutative Property

3 + 5 = 5 + 3

Order does not matter.

---

### Identity Property

7 + 0 = 7

Adding zero changes nothing.

---

## Relationship with Subtraction

Addition and subtraction are inverse operations.

Example:

4 + 3 = 7

7 − 3 = 4

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Bigger number must come first

Incorrect:

2 + 7 is different from 7 + 2

Correct:

Both equal 9.

---

### Plus means "write numbers together"

Incorrect:

2 + 3 = 23

Correct:

2 + 3 = 5

---

### Counting mistakes

Children may skip numbers or count objects twice.

## Teacher Notes

Encourage multiple strategies.

Avoid emphasizing memorization too early.

Develop number sense first.

- fruits
