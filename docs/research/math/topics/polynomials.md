# Polynomials

## Purpose

Help children understand algebraic expressions involving variables and powers, building the foundation for algebra, graphing, calculus, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

Polynomials are algebraic expressions made of variables, constants, and exponents.

Examples:

3x + 2

x² + 5x + 6

2x³ − x + 7

Polynomials are among the most important objects in algebra.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Understanding Terms

Example:

4x + 3

Contains two terms:

4x

3

---

### Stage 2: Constants and Variables

Example:

5x + 8

Variable:

x

Constant:

8

---

### Stage 3: Coefficients

Example:

7x²

Coefficient:

7

Variable:

x

Exponent:

2

---

### Stage 4: Degree

Example:

3x² + 4x + 1

Highest exponent:

2

Degree:

2

---

### Stage 5: Types of Polynomials

Monomial:

4x

---

Binomial:

x + 5

---

Trinomial:

x² + 3x + 2

---

### Stage 6: Adding Polynomials

Example:

(2x + 3) + (5x + 1)

Answer:

7x + 4

Combine like terms.

---

### Stage 7: Subtracting Polynomials

Example:

(7x + 8) − (3x + 2)

Answer:

4x + 6

---

### Stage 8: Multiplying Polynomials

Example:

x(x + 3)

Answer:

x² + 3x

---

### Stage 9: Factoring

Example:

x² + 5x + 6

Factored form:

(x + 2)(x + 3)

## Relationship with Graphs

Linear polynomials produce straight lines.

Quadratic polynomials produce parabolas.

Higher-degree polynomials produce more complex curves.

---

## Relationship with Functions

Polynomials define many important functions.

Examples:

y = x + 2

y = x²

y = x³

---

## Relationship with Area Models

Example:

(x + 2)(x + 3)

can be represented geometrically using rectangles.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Exponents Apply to Entire Expressions

Example:

x² + 3

Only x is squared.

---

### Unlike Terms Can Be Combined

Incorrect:

2x + 3x² = 5x³

Correct:

They cannot be combined.

---

### Degree Equals Number of Terms

Degree depends on the highest exponent.

---

### Variables Must Be x

Variables may be:

x

y

a

n

and others.
