# Unit Circle

## Purpose

Help children understand how angles and coordinates are related on a circle, building the foundation for trigonometric functions, periodic motion, physics, and calculus.

---

## Core Concepts

The unit circle is a circle with:

- radius = 1
- center at (0,0)

Every point on the unit circle corresponds to an angle.

The coordinates of a point are related to:

- cosine
- sine

The unit circle connects:

- geometry
- algebra
- trigonometry
- graphing

## Common Misconceptions

### Angles Stop at 360°

Angles continue indefinitely.

Example:

450°

is equivalent to

90°

---

### Radius Changes

The unit circle always has radius 1.

---

### Coordinates Are Random

Coordinates follow precise trigonometric relationships.

---

### Degrees and Radians Are Different Concepts

They are simply different units for measuring angles.
