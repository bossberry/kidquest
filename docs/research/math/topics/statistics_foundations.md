# Statistics Foundations

## Purpose

Help children understand how data can be collected, summarized, interpreted, and used to make decisions, building the foundation for probability, data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.

---

## Core Concepts

Statistics is the study of data.

Statistics helps answer questions such as:

- What is typical?
- How much variation exists?
- What patterns are present?
- What conclusions can we draw?

Statistics transforms raw information into useful knowledge.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Collecting Data

Examples:

- favorite colors
- heights
- ages

Children learn that information can be organized.

---

### Stage 2: Organizing Data

Data may be represented using:

- tables
- charts
- graphs

---

### Stage 3: Describing Data

Questions include:

- Which value occurs most often?
- What is the average?
- How spread out are the values?

---

### Stage 4: Mean

Example:

Scores:

5, 6, 7

Mean:

6

The mean represents the average value.

---

### Stage 5: Median

The median is the middle value.

Example:

3, 5, 7

Median:

5

---

### Stage 6: Mode

The mode is the most frequent value.

Example:

2, 2, 3, 5

Mode:

2

---

### Stage 7: Range

Range measures spread.

Example:

Maximum:

10

Minimum:

3

Range:

7

---

### Stage 8: Variability

Not all data sets are equally spread out.

Statistics studies variation.

---

## Mean

Mean equals:

Total sum

divided by

Number of values

The mean is sensitive to extreme values.

---

## Median

Median represents the center of ordered data.

Outliers affect the median less than the mean.

---

## Mode

Mode identifies the most common value.

A data set may have:

- one mode
- multiple modes
- no mode

---

## Range

Range measures the difference between:

largest value

and

smallest value

---

## Population and Sample

Population:

Entire group.

Sample:

Part of the group.

Samples help estimate populations.
