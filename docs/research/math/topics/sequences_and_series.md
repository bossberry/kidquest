# Sequences and Series

## Purpose

Help children understand patterns of numbers and the sums of those patterns, building the foundation for algebra, precalculus, calculus, finance, and scientific modeling.

---

## Core Concepts

A sequence is an ordered list of numbers that follows a rule.

Examples:

1, 2, 3, 4, 5

2, 4, 6, 8, 10

A series is the sum of the terms in a sequence.

Example:

1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5

Patterns in sequences help describe change and predict future values.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Recognizing Patterns

Examples:

2, 4, 6, 8

5, 10, 15, 20

Children identify rules.

---

### Stage 2: Terms

Example:

3, 6, 9, 12

First term:

3

Fourth term:

12

Each number is called a term.

---

### Stage 3: Arithmetic Sequences

Example:

4, 7, 10, 13

Common difference:

3

Each term increases by the same amount.

---

### Stage 4: Geometric Sequences

Example:

2, 6, 18, 54

Common ratio:

3

Each term is multiplied by the same number.

---

### Stage 5: Infinite Sequences

Examples:

1, 2, 3, 4, ...

2, 4, 8, 16, ...

Patterns may continue forever.

---

### Stage 6: Series

Sequence:

1, 2, 3, 4

Series:

1 + 2 + 3 + 4 = 10

Series represent accumulated values.

---

### Stage 7: Arithmetic Series

Example:

2 + 4 + 6 + 8

Arithmetic series arise from arithmetic sequences.

---

### Stage 8: Geometric Series

Example:

1 + 2 + 4 + 8

Geometric series arise from geometric sequences.

## Common Misconceptions

### Sequences Must Increase

Some sequences decrease.

Example:

10, 8, 6, 4

---

### Series and Sequences Are the Same

Sequences list numbers.

Series add numbers.

---

### Patterns Always Continue Forever

Finite sequences eventually stop.

---

### Geometric Means Geometric Shapes

Geometric sequences involve multiplication patterns.
