# Money

## Purpose

Help children understand the value of money, recognize coins and bills, and develop the foundation for financial literacy, arithmetic, and real-world problem solving.

---

## Core Concepts

Money is used to:

- buy goods
- pay for services
- save for future needs
- compare values

Children learn that money has value and different amounts can be combined in multiple ways.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Recognizing Coins and Bills

Children identify common forms of money.

Examples:

- coins
- banknotes

---

### Stage 2: Understanding Value

Different coins and bills represent different amounts.

Example:

10 is greater than 5.

---

### Stage 3: Counting Money

Examples:

5 + 5 = 10

10 + 10 = 20

Children combine values.

---

### Stage 4: Multiple Ways to Make the Same Amount

Example:

20

Can be made by:

- 10 + 10
- 5 + 5 + 5 + 5

Different combinations can represent the same value.

---

### Stage 5: Comparing Prices

Examples:

15 baht and 25 baht

25 baht is more expensive.

---

### Stage 6: Buying and Paying

Example:

Toy costs 30 baht.

Pay with:

20 + 10

Total:

30 baht

---

### Stage 7: Making Change

Example:

Price:

35 baht

Paid:

50 baht

Change:

15 baht

---

### Stage 8: Saving Money

Children understand:

- spending
- saving
- planning

## Coins and Bills

Countries use different money systems.

Examples include:

- coins
- banknotes

Both represent value.

---

## Comparing Values

Examples:

20 > 10

50 > 25

100 > 50

Children learn to compare amounts.

---

## Estimation

Example:

Items cost:

18

22

Estimate:

20 + 20

≈ 40

Estimation helps with shopping decisions.

---

## Relationship with Arithmetic

### Addition

Combining money.

Example:

10 + 20 = 30

---

### Subtraction

Finding change.

Example:

50 − 35 = 15

---

### Multiplication

Buying multiple items.

Example:

4 toys costing 10 each

4 × 10 = 40

---

### Division

Sharing money equally.

Example:

20 shared among 4 children

Answer:

5 each

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Bigger Coins Always Mean Greater Value

Physical size does not determine value.

---

### One Coin Is Always Better Than Several Coins

Different combinations can represent the same amount.

---

### Forgetting Units

25 and 25 baht are not the same as 25 dollars.

---

### Incorrect Change Calculation

Children may subtract incorrectly.

Using counting-up strategies can help.
