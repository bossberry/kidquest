# Volume and Capacity

## Purpose

Help children understand how much space objects occupy and how much liquid containers can hold, building the foundation for geometry, measurement, science, and real-world problem solving.

---

## Core Concepts

Volume measures the amount of space occupied by a three-dimensional object.

Capacity measures how much a container can hold.

Although closely related, volume and capacity are different concepts.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Volume and Capacity Are Identical

They are related but not identical.

---

### Bigger Objects Always Have Greater Capacity

Shape matters.

Tall containers may not hold more.

---

### Forgetting Units

24 cm³ is different from 24 cm.

---

### Counting Faces Instead of Cubes

Volume measures cubic units, not surfaces.

---

### Confusing Area and Volume

Area measures surfaces.

Volume measures space.
