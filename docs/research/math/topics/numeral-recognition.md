# Numeral Recognition

## Purpose

Help children recognize, identify, read, and write numerals and connect number symbols with quantities.

---

## Core Concepts

Numerals are symbols used to represent numbers.

Examples:

- 0
- 1
- 2
- 3
- 10
- 25
- 100

Children must understand that numerals represent quantities, not just shapes.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Numerals 0–5

Children recognize:

- 0
- 1
- 2
- 3
- 4
- 5

Match numerals to objects.

Example:

3 → 🍎🍎🍎

---

### Stage 2: Numerals 0–10

Children identify:

0–10

Read numbers aloud.

Count objects accurately.

---

### Stage 3: Numerals 11–20

Recognize teen numbers.

Examples:

11

14

17

20

Understand that teen numbers represent ten plus extra ones.

---

### Stage 4: Numerals 21–100

Recognize larger numbers.

Examples:

25

38

52

100

---

### Stage 5: Multi-Digit Numbers

Examples:

125

349

1,000

Recognize that numbers consist of multiple digits.

## Number Formation

Children practice writing numerals correctly.

Examples:

- tracing
- copying
- writing independently

Focus on:

- direction
- size
- orientation

---

## Visual Discrimination

Distinguish similar numerals.

Examples:

6 and 9

2 and 5

1 and 7

3 and 8

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Reversed Digits

Examples:

6 ↔ 9

2 ↔ 5

3 ↔ E

Mirror writing is developmentally normal.

---

### Confusing Quantity and Size

Children may think:

A bigger object means a bigger number.

Example:

3 large apples > 5 small apples

Correct:

Five objects represent a larger quantity.

---

### Counting Errors

Children may:

- skip numbers
- double count
- mismatch one object to multiple number words

---

### Recognizing Shape Without Meaning

Children may memorize symbols without understanding quantity.

Recognition should always connect to counting.

## Teacher Notes

Recognition should develop alongside quantity understanding.

Avoid teaching numerals purely through memorization.

Use concrete objects frequently.

---

## Parent Notes

Integrate numbers into daily life.

Examples:

- counting toys
- elevator buttons
- page numbers
- shopping prices

Make learning playful rather than drill-based.
