# Derivatives

## Purpose

Help children understand rates of change and instantaneous motion, building the foundation for differential calculus, optimization, physics, engineering, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

A derivative measures how rapidly a quantity changes.

Derivatives describe:

- speed
- growth
- steepness
- instantaneous change

The derivative is one of the central ideas of calculus.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Changing Quantities

Examples:

- temperature
- distance
- height

Children recognize that quantities can change.

---

### Stage 2: Average Rate of Change

Example:

Distance traveled:

100 km

Time:

2 hours

Average speed:

50 km/h

---

### Stage 3: Steepness of Graphs

Steeper graphs represent faster change.

Slope measures steepness.

---

### Stage 4: Instantaneous Change

Average speed differs from speed at a particular moment.

Derivatives measure change at an instant.

---

### Stage 5: Tangent Lines

A tangent line touches a curve at one point.

Its slope represents the derivative.

---

### Stage 6: Positive and Negative Derivatives

Positive derivative:

Increasing function

Negative derivative:

Decreasing function

---

### Stage 7: Zero Derivative

Horizontal tangent.

Possible maximum or minimum point.

---

### Stage 8: Higher Derivatives

Velocity is the derivative of position.

Acceleration is the derivative of velocity.

## Rates of Change

Examples:

- speed
- population growth
- cooling
- inflation

Derivatives describe these changes.

---

## Tangent Lines

The slope of the tangent line equals the derivative.

Steeper tangent lines correspond to larger derivatives.

---

## Positive Derivatives

Graphs rise from left to right.

Function values increase.

---

## Negative Derivatives

Graphs fall from left to right.

Function values decrease.

---

## Zero Derivative

The graph is temporarily flat.

Possible turning point.

---

## Relationship with Limits

Derivatives are defined using limits.

Limits are the foundation of differentiation.

## Common Misconceptions

### Derivatives Only Apply to Motion

Derivatives apply to any changing quantity.

---

### Zero Derivative Means Zero Function

The function value may be nonzero.

---

### Steeper Graph Means Larger y-Values

Steepness measures change, not height.

---

### Derivatives Exist Everywhere

Some functions are not differentiable at certain points.
