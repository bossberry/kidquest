# Graphing Linear Relationships

## Purpose

Help children understand how equations, tables, and graphs are connected, building the foundation for algebra, functions, and calculus.

---

## Core Concepts

Linear relationships describe situations where quantities change at a constant rate.

Their graphs form straight lines.

Linear relationships connect:

- equations
- tables
- ordered pairs
- graphs

---

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Input-Output Tables

Example:

| Input | Output |
| ----- | ------ |
| 1     | 2      |
| 2     | 4      |
| 3     | 6      |
| 4     | 8      |

Rule:

Multiply by 2

---

### Stage 2: Ordered Pairs

From the table:

(1,2)

(2,4)

(3,6)

(4,8)

Ordered pairs represent points.

---

### Stage 3: Plotting Points

Points are placed on the coordinate plane.

Patterns become visible.

---

### Stage 4: Connecting Points

The points form a straight line.

Straight lines indicate linear relationships.

---

### Stage 5: Understanding Constant Change

Example:

y = 2x

Every increase of 1 in x causes y to increase by 2.

---

### Stage 6: Slope

Slope describes steepness.

Example:

y = 3x

Slope = 3

---

### Stage 7: Intercepts

Example:

y = x + 4

The graph crosses the y-axis at:

4

---

### Stage 8: Comparing Linear Relationships

Example:

y = x

y = 3x

The second line is steeper.

---

## Tables, Equations, and Graphs

A linear relationship can be represented in three ways:

### Table

| Input | Output |
| ----- | ------ |
| 1     | 3      |
| 2     | 5      |
| 3     | 7      |

---

### Equation

y = 2x + 1

---

### Graph

Straight line through the corresponding points.

---

## Relationship with Slope

Slope measures the rate of change.

Example:

y = 4x

Slope = 4

---

## Relationship with Functions

Linear relationships are functions.

Each input has exactly one output.

---

## Relationship with Real Life

Examples:

- hourly wages

- savings growth

---

## Common Misconceptions

### All Graphs Are Linear

Curved graphs are not linear.

---

### Steeper Means Higher

Steepness depends on slope, not height.

---

### Points Can Be Connected Randomly

Points must follow the rule.

---

### Linear Relationships Must Pass Through Zero

Example:

y = x + 5

does not pass through the origin.
