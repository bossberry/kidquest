# Data and Graphs

## Purpose

Help children collect, organize, represent, and interpret information, building the foundation for statistics, probability, and data science.

---

## Core Concepts

Data is information that can be collected and analyzed.

Graphs help people organize and understand data visually.

Examples:

- favorite colors
- number of pets
- daily temperatures
- sports scores

---

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Sorting Objects

Children classify objects into groups.

Examples:

- colors
- shapes
- animals

---

### Stage 2: Tally Marks

Example:

Dogs

||||

Cats

|||

Tally marks help count frequencies.

---

### Stage 3: Tables

| Fruit  | Number |
| ------ | -----: |
| Apple  |      4 |
| Banana |      2 |
| Orange |      3 |

Tables organize information.

---

### Stage 4: Picture Graphs

🍎 = 1 apple

🍎🍎🍎🍎

Represents:

4 apples

Picture graphs are easy for young children to interpret.

---

### Stage 5: Bar Graphs

Example:

Favorite Colors

Blue: ████

Red: ███

Green: ██

Bar heights show frequencies.

---

### Stage 6: Reading Graphs

Questions:

- Which category has the most?
- Which category has the least?
- How many altogether?

---

### Stage 7: Comparing Data

Example:

Dogs = 6

Cats = 4

Difference:

2

Children use arithmetic with data.

---

### Stage 8: Collecting Data

Examples:

- surveys
- observations
- experiments

---

## Types of Graphs

### Picture Graph

Uses symbols or pictures.

---

### Bar Graph

Uses bars to show frequencies.

---

### Line Graph

Shows changes over time.

Example:

Daily temperatures.

---

### Pie Chart

Shows parts of a whole.

Useful for percentages.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Taller Bars Mean Bigger Objects

Bars represent quantity, not size.

---

### Ignoring Labels

Axes and categories are important.

---

### Counting Pictures Incorrectly

Always check the graph key.

---

### Confusing Categories

Data must be grouped correctly.

## Teacher Notes

Begin with concrete objects and picture graphs.

Emphasize interpretation rather than drawing.

---

## Parent Notes

Use everyday questions.

Examples:

- favorite fruits
- number of books
- weekly activities

Ask:

"What does the graph tell us?"
