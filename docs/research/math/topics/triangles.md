# Triangles

## Purpose

Help children understand the properties, classifications, and relationships of triangles, building the foundation for geometry, trigonometry, engineering, and spatial reasoning.

---

## Core Concepts

A triangle is a polygon with:

- 3 sides
- 3 vertices
- 3 angles

Triangles are one of the most important shapes in mathematics because many complex shapes can be divided into triangles.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Recognizing Triangles

Children identify triangles in:

- signs
- roofs
- slices of pizza

---

### Stage 2: Counting Sides and Vertices

Triangles always have:

- 3 sides
- 3 vertices

---

### Stage 3: Comparing Triangles

Examples:

Small triangles

Large triangles

Rotated triangles

Orientation and size do not change the shape.

---

### Stage 4: Classifying by Side Length

#### Equilateral Triangle

All three sides are equal.

All angles are equal.

---

#### Isosceles Triangle

Two sides are equal.

Two angles are equal.

---

#### Scalene Triangle

All sides are different.

All angles are different.

---

### Stage 5: Classifying by Angle Type

#### Acute Triangle

All angles are less than 90°.

---

#### Right Triangle

One angle equals 90°.

---

#### Obtuse Triangle

One angle is greater than 90°.

---

### Stage 6: Interior Angle Sum

The three angles of every triangle add to:

180°

This is one of the most important properties of triangles.

---

### Stage 7: Base and Height

Triangles have:

- a base
- a height

These measurements are used to determine area.

---

### Stage 8: Area of Triangles

Area equals:

Half × base × height

Triangles occupy half the area of a rectangle with the same base and height.

---

## Types of Triangles

### Equilateral Triangle

Properties:

- 3 equal sides
- 3 equal angles

---

### Isosceles Triangle

Properties:

- 2 equal sides
- 2 equal angles

---

### Scalene Triangle

Properties:

- no equal sides

---

### Right Triangle

Contains one right angle.

Right triangles are fundamental in trigonometry.

---

## Relationship with Angles

Every triangle contains:

- 3 angles

Their sum is always:

180°

---

## Relationship with Polygons

Many polygons can be divided into triangles.

Examples:

Rectangle → 2 triangles

Pentagon → 3 triangles

Hexagon → 4 triangles

---

## Relationship with Area

Triangles and rectangles are closely related.

Area of a triangle:

½ × base × height

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Orientation Changes the Triangle

Rotating a triangle does not change its type.

---

### Equal Sides Mean Equal Angles Only Sometimes

In triangles, equal sides correspond to equal angles.

---

### Larger Triangles Have Larger Angles

Size does not determine angle measures.

---

### All Triangles Look the Same

Triangles can have many different forms.
