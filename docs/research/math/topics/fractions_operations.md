# Fraction Operations

## Purpose

Help children understand how to add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions, building the foundation for algebra, ratios, percentages, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

Fractions represent parts of a whole.

Operations with fractions involve understanding relationships between parts, not just applying procedures.

Examples:

- addition
- subtraction
- multiplication
- division

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Equivalent Fractions

Examples:

1/2 = 2/4

3/6 = 1/2

Equivalent fractions represent the same quantity.

---

### Stage 2: Comparing Fractions

Examples:

1/2 > 1/4

3/4 > 2/4

Children use visual models and common denominators.

---

### Stage 3: Adding Fractions with the Same Denominator

Example:

1/5 + 2/5

= 3/5

Denominators remain unchanged.

---

### Stage 4: Subtracting Fractions with the Same Denominator

Example:

4/7 − 2/7

= 2/7

---

### Stage 5: Common Denominators

Example:

1/2 + 1/4

Convert:

1/2 = 2/4

2/4 + 1/4

= 3/4

---

### Stage 6: Mixed Numbers

Example:

1 1/2 + 2 1/2

= 4

Children connect whole numbers and fractions.

---

### Stage 7: Multiplying Fractions

Example:

1/2 × 1/3

= 1/6

Multiplication finds part of a part.

---

### Stage 8: Dividing Fractions

Example:

1/2 ÷ 1/4

= 2

Division asks:

"How many fourths are contained in one half?"

---

## Addition of Fractions

### Same Denominator

Example:

3/8 + 2/8

= 5/8

---

### Different Denominators

Example:

1/3 + 1/6

Convert:

1/3 = 2/6

2/6 + 1/6

= 3/6

= 1/2

---

## Subtraction of Fractions

Example:

5/6 − 1/6

= 4/6

= 2/3

---

## Multiplication of Fractions

Example:

2/3 × 3/4

= 6/12

= 1/2

Multiplication often produces smaller quantities.

---

## Division of Fractions

Example:

2/3 ÷ 1/3

= 2

Use reciprocals.

---

## Simplifying Fractions

Example:

6/8

= 3/4

Fractions should be written in simplest form whenever possible.

---

## Improper Fractions and Mixed Numbers

Improper fraction:

7/4

Mixed number:

1 3/4

Children learn to convert between forms.

---

## Relationship with Decimals

Example:

---

## Relationship with Percentages

Example:

1/4

= 25%

1/2

= 50%

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Adding Denominators

Incorrect:

1/3 + 1/3 = 2/6

Correct:

2/3

---

### Larger Denominator Means Larger Fraction

Incorrect:

1/8 > 1/4

Correct:

1/8 is smaller.

---

### Multiplication Always Makes Numbers Larger

Example:

1/2 × 1/2

= 1/4

---

### Division Always Makes Numbers Smaller

Example:

1/2 ÷ 1/4

= 2

## Teacher Notes

Emphasize visual models and meaning.

Avoid teaching procedures without understanding.

Develop conceptual foundations before memorization.
