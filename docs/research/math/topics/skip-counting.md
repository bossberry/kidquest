# Skip Counting

## Purpose

Help children recognize patterns in numbers and develop the foundation for multiplication, division, fractions, time, and money.

---

## Core Concepts

Skip counting means counting by numbers other than one.

Examples:

By 2s:

2, 4, 6, 8, 10

By 5s:

5, 10, 15, 20

By 10s:

10, 20, 30, 40

Skip counting helps children recognize patterns and quantities efficiently.

## Patterns in Skip Counting

### By 2s

Alternates between even numbers.

Example:

2, 4, 6, 8, 10

---

### By 5s

Numbers end in:

0 or 5

Example:

5, 10, 15, 20, 25

---

### By 10s

The ones digit stays the same.

Example:

3, 13, 23, 33

## Common Misconceptions

### Memorizing Without Understanding

Children should recognize patterns, not just recite sequences.

---

### Losing Track

Example:

2, 4, 6, 10

Missing:

8

---

### Confusing Skip Counting with Ordinary Counting

Children may accidentally count by ones.

---

### Believing Skip Counting Must Start at Zero

Example:

Count by fives from 3:

3

8

13

18

23

Skip counting can start from any number.

## Teacher Notes

Focus on patterns and relationships.

Use movement and rhythm.

Avoid teaching skip counting as pure memorization.
