# Systems of Equations

## Purpose

Help children understand how multiple equations can work together to describe relationships, building the foundation for algebra, graphing, and mathematical modeling.

---

## Core Concepts

A system of equations consists of two or more equations considered together.

The solution to a system is a value that satisfies all equations simultaneously.

Systems can be represented using:

- equations
- tables
- graphs

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Review of Simple Equations

Example:

x + 3 = 7

Solution:

x = 4

Children understand solving one equation.

---

### Stage 2: Two Variables

Example:

x + y = 8

Many pairs satisfy the equation:

(1,7)

(2,6)

(3,5)

---

### Stage 3: Two Equations Together

Example:

x + y = 8

x = 3

The pair satisfying both equations is:

(3,5)

---

### Stage 4: Graphical Interpretation

Each equation forms a line.

The solution occurs where the lines intersect.

---

### Stage 5: Unique Solutions

Example:

y = x

y = x + 2

These lines never meet.

Some systems have no solutions.

---

### Stage 6: Infinite Solutions

Example:

y = 2x

2y = 4x

These equations represent the same line.

Every point on the line is a solution.

---

### Stage 7: Solving by Substitution

Example:

x = 5

x + y = 10

Substitute:

5 + y = 10

Solution:

y = 5

---

### Stage 8: Solving by Elimination

Example:

x + y = 10

x − y = 2

Adding the equations gives:

2x = 12

Solution:

x = 6

y = 4

---

## Graphical Interpretation

Solutions correspond to intersection points.

Possible outcomes:

### One Solution

Lines intersect once.

---

### No Solution

Parallel lines never intersect.

---

### Infinite Solutions

Coincident lines overlap completely.

---

## Relationship with Graphing

Each equation produces a graph.

The intersection represents the common solution.

---

## Relationship with Functions

Systems compare multiple functions.

Example:

Revenue function

## Common Misconceptions

### Solve Equations Separately

The solution must satisfy both equations.

---

### All Systems Have One Solution

Some have none.

Some have infinitely many.

---

### Intersection Means Crossing Anywhere

The exact point matters.

---

### Parallel Lines Intersect Eventually

Parallel lines never meet.
