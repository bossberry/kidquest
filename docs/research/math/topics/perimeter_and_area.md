# Perimeter and Area

## Purpose

Help children understand how to measure the boundary and surface of shapes, building the foundation for geometry, multiplication, and real-world measurement.

---

## Core Concepts

Perimeter measures the distance around a shape.

Area measures the amount of surface inside a shape.

These are different concepts and should not be confused.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Understanding Inside and Outside

Children distinguish:

- inside a shape
- outside a shape

This prepares them for area and perimeter.

---

### Stage 2: Measuring Boundaries

Example:

Walk around the edge of a playground.

Children understand perimeter as distance around.

---

### Stage 3: Counting Square Units

Example:

⬜⬜⬜

⬜⬜⬜

Area:

6 square units

Children understand area through counting.

---

### Stage 4: Finding Perimeter by Addition

Example:

Rectangle

Sides:

4, 3, 4, 3

Perimeter:

4 + 3 + 4 + 3 = 14 units

---

### Stage 5: Area of Rectangles

Example:

Length = 5

Width = 3

Area:

5 × 3 = 15 square units

---

### Stage 6: Relationship with Multiplication

Rows:

4

Columns:

6

Area:

4 × 6 = 24 square units

Multiplication provides an efficient method.

---

### Stage 7: Comparing Shapes

Two shapes may have:

- the same area
- different perimeters

or

- the same perimeter
- different areas

---

### Stage 8: Irregular Shapes

Find area by counting squares.

Estimate partial squares when necessary.

---

## Perimeter

Perimeter is the total distance around a shape.

Examples:

Fence around a garden

Running track

Picture frame

---

## Area

Area measures surface coverage.

Examples:

Floor tiles

Carpet

Painting a wall

---

## Units

### Perimeter Units

Examples:

- centimeters
- meters

---

### Area Units

Examples:

- square centimeters (cm²)
- square meters (m²)

Area always uses square units.

---

## Relationship Between Area and Multiplication

Example:

3 rows

4 columns

Area:

12 square units

Arrays connect multiplication and area.

---

## Common Misconceptions

### Confusing Perimeter and Area

Perimeter measures around.

Area measures inside.

---

### Forgetting Units

Example:

20 cm² is different from 20 cm.

---

### Multiplying for Perimeter

Perimeter often requires addition.

---

### Adding for Area

Area of rectangles is found using multiplication.

---

### Assuming Larger Perimeter Means Larger Area

Two shapes can have the same perimeter but different areas.

## Teacher Notes

Develop understanding through physical experiences.

Use grid paper extensively.

Emphasize the difference between perimeter and area.

Avoid introducing formulas before concepts are understood.
