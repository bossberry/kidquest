# Differential Equations

## Purpose

Help children understand how changing quantities can be described mathematically, building the foundation for physics, biology, engineering, economics, and advanced mathematics.

---

## Core Concepts

A differential equation is an equation involving:

- a function
- one or more derivatives

Differential equations describe how quantities change over time.

Many natural laws are expressed as differential equations.

## Learning Progression

### Stage 1: Review of Derivatives

Derivatives measure rates of change.

Examples:

- speed
- acceleration
- population growth

---

### Stage 2: Describing Change

Example:

The rate at which water fills a tank.

Change itself becomes the object of study.

---

### Stage 3: Equations with Derivatives

Example:

Rate of growth depends on current size.

The equation involves both a quantity and its derivative.

---

### Stage 4: Solutions

Solutions to differential equations are functions.

Unlike algebraic equations, answers are often entire curves.

---

### Stage 5: Initial Conditions

Initial values determine particular solutions.

Example:

Starting population.

---

### Stage 6: Growth and Decay

Examples:

- bacteria growth
- radioactive decay
- investment growth

---

### Stage 7: Motion

Relationships among:

- position
- velocity
- acceleration

can be expressed using differential equations.

---

### Stage 8: Mathematical Models

Many systems in science and engineering are modeled by differential equations.

---

## Growth Models

Examples:

---

## Decay Models

Examples:

## Motion

Position changes produce velocity.

Velocity changes produce acceleration.

Differential equations describe these relationships.

---

## Equilibrium

Some systems stabilize over time.

Examples:

- predator-prey populations
- chemical reactions

## Relationship with Derivatives

Differential equations are built from derivatives.

Without derivatives, differential equations cannot exist.

---

## Relationship with Integrals

Integrals help solve differential equations.

Differentiation and integration work together.

## Common Misconceptions

### Solutions Are Always Numbers

Solutions are often functions.

---

### Differential Equations Belong Only to Physics

They appear in many fields.

---

### Rates of Change Are Constant

Many systems change at changing rates.

---

### Exact Solutions Always Exist

Some differential equations require approximations.
